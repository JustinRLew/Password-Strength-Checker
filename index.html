NIST Password Guidelines (As of 2024)

As of 2024, the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has updated its guidelines on password complexity and management in Special Publication 800-63B (Digital Identity Guidelines: Authentication and Lifecycle Management). Here are the key practices recommended by NIST regarding password complexity:

  • Length over Complexity: Minimum password length is 8 characters for user-chosen passwords. Longer passphrases (16 characters) are more effective than arbitrary complexity rules.
  • Avoid Mandatory Complexity Requirements: NIST discourages requiring a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. It often leads to weaker, predictable passwords.
  • Ban Common Passwords: Systems should block the use of common passwords found in breach databases or that contain dictionary words or user information.
  • No Mandatory Periodic Changes: NIST advises against periodic password changes unless thereâ€™s evidence of compromise.
  • Password Reuse Restrictions: Prevent reuse of recent passwords to protect against the reuse of compromised passwords.
  • Allow Password Managers: Encourage the use of password managers to generate and store strong, unique passwords.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): NIST recommends MFA for stronger security. MFA adds extra protection with something the user knows (password), has (token), or is (biometric).
  • Avoid Knowledge-Based Authentication (KBA): Do not use security questions for password recovery. KBA is insecure and easily guessable.
